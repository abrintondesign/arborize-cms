How I Got Into This Work

I've been working with trees for most of my adult life. What started as hands-on work evolved into something I wanted to understand properly — not just how to cut, but why trees fail, how they respond to pruning, what makes a removal genuinely safe versus just fast.

That's what drove me to pursue ISA certification and later earn TRAQ certification as well. The International Society of Arboriculture's exam covers tree biology, soil science, risk assessment, pruning standards, disease identification, and safe work practices. TRAQ adds a formal framework for evaluating tree risk when safety, liability, permits or insurance are part of the conversation.

I keep both credentials current. It means that when I assess a tree and tell you something, there's a tested professional standard behind that judgment — whether the job needs pruning, removal or a written risk-based recommendation.

Why I Work This Way

I built Arborize Ltd. around a simple idea: do the work right, be straight with people, and the business takes care of itself. Most of my work comes from referrals. A neighbour sees a job, it looks good, they call me next. That's the only marketing that actually works — and it only works if every job earns it.

I don't subcontract. When I take on a job, I'm accountable for the outcome in a direct way that a company sending a crew you've never met simply isn't. You know who's showing up, and so do I.

I also won't tell you a tree needs to come down if it doesn't. Or that it needs treatment if treatment isn't warranted. A few times a year I walk a property, tell someone their tree is fine, and drive away without a dollar. That's the job done right. It's also why people refer me to their neighbours.

How I Approach the Work

Safety comes before everything else. Tree work injures and kills people every year — mostly people who underestimated what they were dealing with. I plan every job before I start it: rigging, escape routes, what's underneath, what's overhead. If the plan needs to change on-site, it changes. The planning doesn't get skipped.

I'd rather take an extra hour and do the job right than rush it. Fast tree removal that damages property or leaves a tree structurally compromised isn't a service — it's a problem deferred to next year.

The site gets cleaned before I leave. Brush chipped, stump ground if it's in scope, wood handled. You shouldn't have to do anything after I go.

I quote jobs in writing after seeing them in person. What I write down is what you pay. No surprises on the invoice, no line items that weren't discussed.
