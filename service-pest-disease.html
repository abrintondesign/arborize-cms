Common Tree Pests and Diseases in Simcoe County

Several pests and diseases are actively causing tree decline and loss across Simcoe County and the broader Barrie, Alliston, and Bradford area. Knowing what you're dealing with is the first step — treatment depends entirely on accurate identification.

Emerald Ash Borer

EAB has devastated ash tree populations across Ontario. If you have ash trees and haven't had them assessed, it's worth knowing what stage they're at. Treatment is possible in earlier stages but the window closes. Trees that are too far gone are better removed than treated — I'll tell you honestly which category yours fall into.

Spongy Moth (Formerly Gypsy Moth)

Spongy moth defoliation is cyclical but severe in parts of Simcoe County. One year of heavy defoliation doesn't necessarily kill a tree, but two or three consecutive years on an already stressed tree often will. Timing of treatment matters — it has to happen when the caterpillars are young and actively feeding.

Tent Caterpillar

Eastern tent caterpillar and forest tent caterpillar both cause visible defoliation, particularly on fruit trees, maples, and oaks. Most healthy trees recover from a single infestation, but repeated years of stress can weaken them significantly.

Fungal Disease and Canker

Dutch elm disease, various canker diseases, and fungal infections like tar spot or verticillium wilt all show up in Simcoe County. Some are manageable, some aren't. Accurate diagnosis — not just a visual guess — makes the difference between effective treatment and wasted money.

Why a Licensed Applicator Matters

In Ontario, applying pesticides to trees requires a licence. It's not optional and it's not a formality — it means the person applying the treatment has been trained in safe handling, environmental impact, appropriate products, and application methods.

I hold an Ontario Pesticide Applicator licence. If someone offers to treat your trees without one, that's a problem regardless of how reasonable the price looks.

This matters enough to say directly. Some trees are too far gone to save, and treating them is a waste of your money. Some problems don't respond to the treatments homeowners expect. I'd rather tell you that upfront than have you spend money on something ineffective.

If treatment is the right call, I'll explain exactly what it involves, what it costs, and what realistic outcome you can expect.
