How I Work

Why Homeowners in Simcoe County Call Me Back

Plenty of people offer tree work. Fewer are ISA certified, properly insured, and honest enough to walk away if the job doesn't need doing.

1

Credentials You Can Actually Check

ISA certification is a real exam and TRAQ adds formal tree risk assessment training on top of that. Both are verifiable. When I tell you a tree is safe, needs pruning or should come down, that's a professional judgment — not a guess.

2

The Site Is Clean When I Leave

Brush chipped, logs handled, stump ground if it's in the scope. I don't consider the job done until the yard looks right. That's included — not an add-on.

3

I'll Tell You If the Work Isn't Needed

If a tree can be saved, I say so. If removal is the right call, I explain why. You get a straight answer — not a recommendation built around a bigger invoice.

4

Fully Licensed and Insured

Full liability insurance on every job. Licensed pesticide applicator for pest and disease work. Ask to see the certificate before I start — I bring it every time.

5

The Price I Quote Is What You Pay

I visit the property, look at the actual job, and give you a written number. No phone estimates that shift when I show up, no line items added halfway through.
